## 2. Core Investment Frameworks

### 2.1 Radical Portfolio Theory

Park's signature framework, which he describes as "a bold reimagining of traditional portfolio construction that challenges the long-standing 60/40 model."

**The Problem:** The traditional 60/40 portfolio (60% equities, 40% bonds) was built on the assumption that stocks and bonds are negatively correlated -- equities represent risk/growth, treasuries represent safety. In the post-COVID market, this correlation reversed from negative to positive, destroying the foundational logic of the model.

**The New Framework: 60% Compliance / 40% Resistance**

Rather than risk-on/risk-off, Park proposes:
- **60% Compliance:** Assets that exist within the traditional institutional system (ETFs, public equities, primary residence, GLD)
- **40% Resistance:** Assets that hedge against institutional breakdown (physical gold, self-custodied Bitcoin, offshore assets)

Key distinctions:
- GLD (Gold ETF) = Compliance. Physical gold bars = Resistance.
- Bitcoin ETF = Compliance. Self-custodied Bitcoin = Resistance.
- Primary residence = Compliance. Anonymously owned offshore shelter = Resistance.

> "We must live our lives within institutions for the most part (60%), but we also need to live our private lives away from such institutions, as it is not improbable that they may break during our lifetime."

**Bitcoin's Role:** The natural perfect resilience asset where scarcity and liquidity coexist. While typical non-orthodox advice suggests 1-5% Bitcoin allocation, under Radical Portfolio Theory, a **20-40% allocation** is justified.

**Self-Custody Matters:** While both Bitcoin and Bitcoin ETFs provide exposure during prosperity, in a crisis they represent completely different risks. Even simple rebalancing during a market crash is impossible when the market is closed. ETFs are still "compliant assets."

### 2.2 The Two Bitcoin Thesis (Negative Rho vs. Positive Rho)

Park's most theoretically ambitious framework, borrowing options terminology (rho = sensitivity to interest rate changes) to describe two fundamentally different Bitcoin bull cases.

**Negative Rho Bitcoin (the familiar thesis):**
- Bitcoin performs better when rates fall
- Rates down -> risk up -> Bitcoin up
- The "risk asset" version most investors recognize
- This was the dominant narrative from 2020-2021
- Requires either deeply negative rates ("pay to save") or markets breaking so that traditional cash flows become unreliable

**Positive Rho Bitcoin (the endgame thesis):**
- Bitcoin rising *because* rates are rising, challenging the notion of a stable "risk-free" rate
- Questions the credibility of the monetary order itself
- Park calls this "the mythical elusive perfect holy grail of what Bitcoin is meant to be"

> "What it's undermining is the risk-free rate itself. In that world, we're saying the dollar hegemony is not the dollar hegemony and we are no longer able to price the yield curve in the ways we've known -- that means we need something different... and Bitcoin is that hedge."

**The Regime Transition:** Park frames the current moment as an "awkward middle" between these two regimes. The trillion-dollar question: Does the Fed revert to financial repression (negative-rho), or do debt, geopolitics, and reality force a confrontation with the fiction of the risk-free rate (positive-rho)?

### 2.3 Peacetime Bitcoin vs. Wartime Bitcoin

A complementary framework distinguishing the conditions under which Bitcoin accrues value:

- **Peacetime Bitcoin:** Driven by monetary policy, liquidity cycles, and risk-on sentiment. The familiar narrative of QE, rate cuts, and rising tide lifting all boats.
- **Wartime Bitcoin:** Driven by geopolitical fragmentation, centralization of government control, capital controls, and censorship resistance. The fundamental value proposition of "free money."

> "It's actually not monetary policy that is driving the future outlook for how we're thinking about growth."

Park argues we are transitioning from peacetime to wartime, and that "more accommodative policies may in fact actually not be the catalyst" for Bitcoin's next bull phase. Bitcoin's bid strengthens when the world feels less like peacetime -- when industrial, military, and fiscal policy dominate.

### 2.4 Plaza Accord 2.0

Park's highest-conviction macro trade. In February 2025, he posted:

> "This is the only thing you need to read about tariffs to understand Bitcoin for 2025. This is undoubtedly my highest conviction macro trade for the year: Plaza Accord 2.0 is coming. Bookmark this and revisit as the financial war unravels sending Bitcoin violently higher."

**The Historical Parallel:** In the 1980s, the dollar surged 50%+ against major currencies. The 1985 Plaza Accord coordinated dollar weakening among the US, UK, Germany, France, and Japan. Park argues "you just have to replace Japan with another Asian country today, which is China."

**The Triffin Dilemma:** The reserve currency status creates three problems -- dollar overvaluation, persistent trade deficits, and cheap government borrowing. Park says the US wants to fix the first two while keeping the third.

**Bitcoin Wins Either Way:**
- If dollar weakening succeeds: lower rates stimulate risk appetite, Bitcoin rises
- If tariff war prolongs: economic weakness leads to money printing "on a scale greater than ever before," Bitcoin rises

> "The result is: Bitcoin wins, fiat loses. In either case, Bitcoin will rise."

## 3. Key Bitcoin Theses and Market Views

### 3.1 The Bitcoin ETF Structure Problem

Park has become one of the most articulate critics of how the ETF structure interacts with Bitcoin price discovery.

**The "Grey Window" Theory (February 2026):**
- Authorized Participants (APs) can hedge ETF exposure using futures instead of buying spot Bitcoin
- APs operate within an exemption under Regulation SHO that allows them to short-sell without the normal locate requirements
- This capital efficiency is "indistinguishable from a regulatory arbitrage"
- The shift to in-kind redemptions removed the "structural governor" that previously forced spot buying
- APs can now source Bitcoin through private OTC desks with minimal market impact

> "No firm is explicitly 'manipulating' the market, but the current regulatory framework, designed for traditional assets, is fundamentally at odds with Bitcoin's mission."

**Structured Note Feedback Loops (February 2026):**
- The February 5 "Black Thursday" crash was driven by multi-asset portfolio deleveraging, not crypto-specific factors
- Goldman's prime brokerage desk called Feb 4 one of the worst daily performance events for multi-strat funds (~3.5 z-score, a 0.05% event)
- Structured notes linked to IBIT ($353M notional by end-2025) forced banks to sell as Bitcoin's price dropped
- Short gamma positions compounded downside as dealers had to sell IBIT as hedges updated
- IBIT recorded 2x prior volume highs (10B+) with put-heavy options skew

### 3.2 The Two-Year Cycle Thesis

Park challenges the traditional four-year halving cycle:

> "The dominant force in Bitcoin's boom-bust dynamics is shifting from mining economics to fund-manager economics."

He argues for a new two-year cycle anchored in ETF flows and institutional return hurdles. Investors must track not only the average ETF cost basis but also "the moving average of that P&L by vintage." These rolling profit profiles will become the main "liquidity pressures and circuit breakers" for Bitcoin.

### 3.3 Morgan Stanley's Bitcoin ETF: "The Most Bullish Thing Ever"

When Morgan Stanley filed for its own spot Bitcoin and Solana ETFs, Park called it "the most bullish thing ever":

> "Despite IBIT being the fastest ETF in history to reach $80Bn in AUM, the fact that Morgan Stanley believes a branded product has commercial viability means the market is MUCH bigger than even crypto professionals anticipated."

Key insights:
- Bitcoin ETFs function as **identity products** as much as allocation sleeves -- "socially important just as much as financially important"
- There are virtually no branded gold ETFs, yet multiple firms want branded Bitcoin ETFs
- Morgan Stanley is making a defensive platform economics play to avoid outsourcing economic value
- Target market: ultra-high-net-worth independent investors

> "Every asset manager knows that having a Bitcoin ETF communicates that they are forward thinking, young, and a little edgy."

### 3.4 Young People and the American Dream

Park emphasizes the social dimension of Bitcoin adoption:

> "At the core of it is because Bitcoin is a movement of young people's hearts. If young people stop participating, the institutionalization of Wall Street happening on the back of their investments is also going to come to a halt."

He frames Bitcoin ownership as the new aspiration, describing "wholecoiner status" (owning at least one full Bitcoin) as "the new American Dream" -- replacing suburban homeownership for a generation that views traditional financial goals as unattainable.

### 3.5 Bitcoin's Supply Advantage and the Illiquidity Premium

Park argues that the illiquidity premium -- a foundational assumption in traditional finance -- is breaking down, and Bitcoin benefits most:

> "Because liquidity itself has the ability to produce significant alpha, cryptocurrency markets behave differently."

Unlike illiquid alternatives where you wait years for value creation, Bitcoin's liquidity allows traders to seize volatility-driven opportunities instantly through market making, arbitrage, and short-term positioning.

## 4. Notable Quotes and Analogies

### On Bitcoin's Nature

> "Bitcoin won not because it was an 'exponential growth' story, but because it was an 'exponential loss' story -- the loss of fiat, the loss of trust, the loss of opportunities. It won because it couldn't be shut down."

### On Volatility

> "Bitcoin's upside story historically leans on volatility. If volatility compresses and stays compressed, Bitcoin loses one of the features that has consistently attracted marginal risk capital."

> "For Bitcoin to reach meaningful new highs, we need implied volatility and realized volatility to rise concurrently."

### On the Harvard-Ethereum Analogy

> "The problem with Harvard is that it thinks it's Bitcoin when it's Ethereum."

Park contrasted Bitcoin's rigid, rules-based system with a fixed cap against Ethereum's social-consensus governance model. Harvard claims its admissions scarcity is a natural law, but actually exercises broad discretion -- making it more like Ethereum. The analogy went viral in December 2025 during Elon Musk's crypto discussion.

## 6. Recent Activity (February-March 2026)

### Bear Market Declaration

In a February 2026 podcast appearance on The Pomp Podcast, Park explicitly stated: **"I believe we are indeed in a bear market, and have been for some time."** He noted that global liquidity has reached ~$170 trillion and continues to increase, yet Bitcoin has not participated in the broader risk asset rally. He argues the old liquidity-Bitcoin correlation is "broken."

### February 5 "Black Thursday" Analysis

Park published detailed analysis attributing the February 5 crash (Bitcoin down 13.2% to as low as ~$66,000) to:
- Multi-asset portfolio deleveraging (not crypto-specific)
- Goldman's prime brokerage flagging one of the worst daily performance events for multi-strat funds (~3.5 z-score)
- Structured note hedging feedback loops (IBIT-linked notes, $353M notional)
- Short gamma compounding to the downside

### Kevin Warsh Nomination Response

After Bitcoin dropped to $82,000 on the Warsh nomination news, Park published a Substack post arguing this could mark the bottom. His counterintuitive thesis: Warsh's hawkish policies could accelerate the transition from "negative rho Bitcoin" to "positive rho Bitcoin" by breaking the financial system's foundational assumptions.

### ETF "Grey Window" Post (February 25, 2026)

Published a viral analysis on X explaining why Bitcoin has not reached $150K despite massive ETF inflows. The post dissected how Authorized Participants use Reg SHO exemptions and the shift to in-kind redemptions to decouple inflows from spot buying pressure.

### Silver Warning

Park publicly warned that the current rise in silver is analogous to altcoin rallies -- "driven by liquidity with weak fundamentals" -- and suggested taking profits and shifting to Bitcoin.

### Departure from Bitwise

In early 2026, Park posted his farewell from Bitwise, noting he is "more convicted than ever" and "honored to remain an Advisor, shareholder, and steadfast supporter." He continues as CIO at ProCap BTC, working alongside Anthony Pompliano.

### Venezuela Strategic Reserve Comment

Park floated the provocative idea on X: **"What if Venezuela is the US Strategic Bitcoin Reserve"** -- hinting at geopolitical dimensions of Bitcoin adoption and state-level strategies.

## 7. Summary Assessment

Jeff Park occupies a unique position in the Bitcoin/crypto ecosystem. He is one of the few voices who bridges:

1. **Traditional finance credibility** (Morgan Stanley derivatives, Harvard Management Company, Corbin Capital multi-strat)
2. **Deep crypto conviction** (first bought Bitcoin in 2013, led institutional crypto strategies since 2014)
3. **Theoretical sophistication** (Radical Portfolio Theory, Two Bitcoin Thesis, Peacetime/Wartime framework)
4. **Practical market structure expertise** (ETF plumbing, gamma hedging, structured note mechanics)

His current stance (as of March 2026) is paradoxical but internally consistent: he is bearish short-term (acknowledging a bear market), skeptical of the old liquidity-Bitcoin correlation, critical of ETF structural suppression, yet deeply long-term bullish because he believes Bitcoin's true value proposition (censorship resistance, decentralization, "free money") becomes more important as the world becomes more centralized and fragmented.

He is not a simple Bitcoin maximalist. He engages seriously with risks (quantum computing, protocol governance debates, ETF structural flaws) and acknowledges short-term pain. But his macro framework consistently concludes that Bitcoin "wins" across multiple scenarios -- whether the dollar weakens by design (Plaza Accord 2.0) or the financial system breaks under its own contradictions (positive rho Bitcoin).
