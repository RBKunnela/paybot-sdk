## 1. Chris Dixon (a16z Crypto)

### Background & Career

Chris Dixon is a general partner at Andreessen Horowitz (a16z), where he has been since 2012. He founded and leads a16z crypto, which invests in web3 technologies through **four dedicated funds with more than $7 billion under management**. Before a16z, Dixon was a serial entrepreneur: he co-founded SiteAdvisor (acquired by McAfee) and Hunch (acquired by eBay). He holds a degree in Philosophy from Columbia University and an MBA from Harvard Business School.

He was ranked **#1 on the Forbes Midas List in 2022**, a testament to his track record of identifying transformative technology investments before consensus forms.

### Crypto Thesis: "Read Write Own"

Dixon's foundational thesis is articulated in his January 2024 book *Read Write Own: Building the Next Era of the Internet* (Random House). The title maps the internet's three eras:

- **Read (Web1):** Static websites, one-way information flow
- **Read-Write (Web2):** User-generated content, but platform capture (Facebook, Google, Amazon own the rails)
- **Read-Write-Own (Web3):** Users own their digital assets, identity, and data via blockchain infrastructure

Dixon's core argument: **blockchains provide the only plausible path to sustaining the original vision of the internet as an open platform** that incentivizes creativity and entrepreneurship. He consistently distinguishes between "casino culture" speculation and genuine technological transformation.

> "The internet made information global. Crypto is doing the same for money." -- Chris Dixon

3. **10+ Year Investment Horizons:** a16z crypto structures its funds for extended horizons, allowing bets on experimental ideas that may take a decade+ to mature.

## 2. Kyle Samani (Multicoin Capital)

### Background & Career

Kyle Samani earned a B.S. in Finance and Management from NYU Stern (2008-2012). He has been programming since age 10. After graduating, he briefly worked at his father's medical records company before co-founding **Pristine**, an enterprise software company developing Google Glass applications for surgeons. Pristine raised over $5M in VC and grew to ~30 employees before being acquired by Upskill.

The experience of building on Google Glass -- and then watching Google kill the consumer product in 2015 -- taught Samani the "pain of platform risk," making the idea of open, permissionless platforms deeply appealing. In March 2016, he discovered Ethereum, which became his "first meaningful source of wealth."

In August 2017, Samani co-founded **Multicoin Capital** in Austin, Texas with his NYU Stern friend **Tushar Jain**. The firm became known for making **highly concentrated, non-consensus crypto bets** -- sometimes putting 30-50%+ of a fund into a single asset.

### Crypto Thesis: The Solana Bet

Multicoin Capital invested in Solana's seed round in **May 2018** -- years before the ecosystem gained traction. Samani has published five iterations of his Solana thesis over the years:

| Thesis | Year | Core Argument |
|---|---|---|
| 1st Solana Thesis | 2018 | High-throughput L1 needed for global-scale applications |
| 2nd-4th iterations | 2019-2024 | DeFi, DePIN, payments, developer ecosystem superiority |
| **5th: "Internet Capital Markets"** | Jan 2025 | Solana as the infrastructure for tokenizing all global assets; path from $100B to $1T market cap |

Key positions from the 5th thesis:
- Solana has surpassed Ethereum on most major on-chain metrics (trading volumes, daily active addresses, REV, TEV, DePIN payments)
- **"These systems are financial systems, first and foremost"** -- a recalibration toward DeFi, stablecoins, RWAs, and cross-border payments
- Ethereum's core problem: "not optimized for anything." Solana's monolithic architecture (low latency, high throughput) is better suited for Internet-native capital markets
- Almost all assets will eventually trade on permissionless systems like Solana: global, 24/7, easier for both retail and developers

### DePIN & Attention Economy

Samani was one of the earliest and loudest proponents of **DePIN** (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks). He sees Solana as the leading chain for DePIN payments and token issuance, with projects building real-world infrastructure (wireless networks, sensors, compute) incentivized by blockchain tokens.

### Key Frameworks & Mental Models

1. **Concentrated Conviction:** Making huge, non-consensus bets rather than diversified portfolios
2. **Platform Risk Avoidance:** Open, permissionless systems over walled gardens (learned from Google Glass)
3. **Financial Systems First:** Recognizing that blockchains are fundamentally financial infrastructure
4. **Monolithic > Modular:** Believing Solana's integrated approach beats Ethereum's roll-up-centric scaling

### Forward Industries & SOL Treasury Model

In September 2025, **Forward Industries** received a $1.65 billion PIPE investment from Multicoin Capital, Galaxy Digital, and Jump Crypto to establish a Solana-focused digital asset treasury. Samani co-founded Forward and highlighted Solana's native yields of 8.05% as offering better economics than Bitcoin treasury models (a la MicroStrategy).

### Departure from Multicoin (February 2026)

On **February 5, 2026**, Kyle Samani stepped down from Multicoin Capital after nearly a decade. Co-founder Tushar Jain explained: "Kyle's interests have expanded beyond crypto into other areas of technology, such as AI, longevity, and robotics."

The timing raised questions: Multicoin had recently purchased over $40M in HYPE (Hyperliquid) tokens, fueling speculation about internal strategy disagreements. Despite leaving, Samani stated:

> "After nearly a decade in crypto, I'm more confident than ever that crypto is going to fundamentally rewire the circuitry of finance."

He will stay on as **chairman of Forward** and continue making personal investments in crypto, particularly Solana-related.

## 3. Su Zhu & Kyle Davies (Three Arrows Capital)

### Background

**Three Arrows Capital (3AC)** was a Singapore-based cryptocurrency hedge fund founded in 2012 by **Su Zhu** and **Kyle Davies**, who had been friends since attending Andover prep school in Massachusetts. The fund initially focused on traditional forex arbitrage before pivoting to crypto. At its peak, 3AC managed approximately **$3 billion** in assets and was one of the most influential crypto hedge funds.

Both founders had backgrounds in traditional finance: Zhu worked at Deutsche Bank and Flow Traders, while Davies worked at Credit Suisse. Their academic pedigree (both Columbia graduates) and TradFi experience gave them credibility in institutional circles.

### The Collapse (June 2022)

3AC's collapse was triggered by a series of catastrophic, highly leveraged bets:

1. **LUNA/UST Exposure:** The fund had massive exposure to Terra's LUNA and UST algorithmic stablecoin, which collapsed to zero in May 2022
2. **GBTC Arbitrage:** 3AC exploited the Grayscale Bitcoin Trust (GBTC) premium trade, which turned negative
3. **Excessive Leverage:** The fund borrowed billions to amplify returns, creating cascading liquidation risks
4. **Staked ETH Illiquidity:** Large positions in stETH that couldn't be liquidated quickly enough

On **June 27, 2022**, a British Virgin Islands court ordered 3AC to liquidate. Bankruptcy filings revealed **$3.5 billion in creditors' claims**. The collapse triggered a contagion that took down Voyager Digital, Celsius Network, and contributed to BlockFi's demise.

### Lessons for Crypto Risk Management

The 3AC collapse became the definitive cautionary tale for crypto:

1. **Concentration Risk:** Massive, correlated positions across multiple protocols
2. **Leverage Cascades:** Borrowing from multiple counterparties who all called margin simultaneously
3. **Counterparty Due Diligence Failure:** Lenders like Genesis, Voyager, and BlockFi extended credit without adequate collateral
4. **Regulatory Gaps:** Operating across multiple jurisdictions with minimal oversight
5. **Reflexivity in Algorithmic Stablecoins:** The circular logic of LUNA/UST was a ticking time bomb

### Current Status

**Su Zhu:**
- Arrested at Changi Airport, Singapore on **September 29, 2023**
- Sentenced to **4 months in prison** for failing to cooperate with liquidators
- Released in **December 2023** (early, for good behavior)
- Post-prison, described his time as "quite enjoyable"
- Received a **9-year market ban** from the Monetary Authority of Singapore
- Became involved with the **OX.FUN exchange** and a memecoin project called "Three Arrowz Capitel ($3AC)"

**Kyle Davies:**
- Has **never been imprisoned** despite potential contempt of court
- Publicly stated he does not intend to return to Singapore
- Said he was "in Europe" but "bounced around between Europe and Asia"
- **Showed no remorse**, stating "on a long enough timeline, every company goes bankrupt"
- His wife Kelly Chen was reported selling their Singapore bungalow for **$28.7 million (S$37M)** in May 2025

**Legal Proceedings (2025):**
- BVI court froze **$1.1 billion** in assets of Zhu, Davies, and Kelly Chen (December 2023)
- Singapore Court of Appeal rejected founders' attempt to block financial disclosure orders (June 2025)
- Liquidators (Teneo) estimate creditors will recover **45.74%** of claims
- Creditors are still owed approximately **$3.3 billion**

## 4. Barry Silbert (Digital Currency Group)

### Background & Career

Barry Silbert founded **Digital Currency Group (DCG)** in 2015 after previously founding **SecondMarket** (acquired by Nasdaq in 2015), one of the first regulated platforms for trading private company shares and alternative investments. DCG has been called the "Standard Oil of blockchain" for its sprawling empire of crypto subsidiaries.

|---|---|---|
| **Grayscale Investments** | Crypto asset management ($35B+ AUM) | IPO filing in progress |
| **Genesis Global** | Crypto lending/trading | Bankrupt (Jan 2023) |
| **Foundry** | Crypto mining & staking | Operating |
| **Luno** | Crypto exchange (emerging markets) | Operating |

| **Yuma** | Decentralized AI (Bittensor) | Launched Nov 2024 |
| **Fortitude** | Crypto mining | Launched Jan 2025 |

### The Genesis Crisis

Genesis Global Capital filed for **Chapter 11 bankruptcy** on January 19, 2023, citing over 100,000 creditors and liabilities of $1-10 billion. The collapse was triggered by exposure to 3AC and FTX. The fallout engulfed DCG in litigation:

- **January 2025:** DCG paid the SEC **$38.5 million** for misleading investors over Genesis funds
- **May 2025:** Genesis Global sued DCG in two courts to recover **$2.1 billion** for creditors
- **May 2025:** Legal filings accused Silbert of personally withdrawing **$800 million** before Genesis collapsed
- **August 2025:** DCG countersued to zero out a **$1.1 billion promissory note** it had issued to Genesis
- New York Attorney General's lawsuit accuses Silbert and DCG of hiding losses exceeding **$1 billion**

### GBTC to ETF Transition

Grayscale's conversion of its Bitcoin Trust (GBTC) into a spot Bitcoin ETF in January 2024 was a watershed moment. Grayscale now offers **more than three dozen products** including spot Bitcoin and Ethereum ETFs, diversified crypto funds, and manages over **$35 billion in AUM**.

### Silbert's Departure and Return to Grayscale

Silbert resigned from the Grayscale board in **December 2023** amid the NYAG lawsuit. In a dramatic reversal, he returned as **chairman of Grayscale's board** on **August 4, 2025**, alongside C-suite hires from Bridgewater, Apollo, Goldman Sachs, and Citadel. This coincided with Grayscale's confidential IPO filing.

### The Yuma / Decentralized AI Pivot

Silbert's most significant strategic move has been launching **Yuma** (November 2024), a subsidiary focused on the **Bittensor** decentralized AI network. Key details:

- Silbert serves as **CEO of Yuma**, named after Bittensor's consensus algorithm
- DCG has invested **$105 million** into decentralized AI projects
- Yuma uses a **Y Combinator-style model**: accelerator program + subnet incubator
- Yuma onboarded 8 institutional validator partners in 2025 (BitGo, Copper, Crypto.com)
- Launched **Yuma Asset Management** with a $10M anchor from DCG, offering two fund strategies for subnet token exposure
- Grayscale launched both a Bittensor Trust and a decentralized AI fund (Bittensor = 21% of the latter)

Silbert's boldest claim:

> "Decentralized AI is the next big era of crypto... bigger than Bitcoin." -- Barry Silbert (Fortune Brainstorm Tech, September 2025)

## 5. Mike Novogratz (Galaxy Digital)

### Background & Career

Michael Novogratz is a former **Goldman Sachs partner** and **Fortress Investment Group** macro fund manager who pivoted entirely to crypto in 2017 when he founded **Galaxy Digital**. Before Wall Street, Novogratz served as a helicopter pilot in the U.S. Army and is a Princeton economics graduate. He is also known for his philanthropy and personal flamboyance, including a large LUNA tattoo he got before that token's collapse (which he has publicly acknowledged as a cautionary tale).

As of January 2026, Forbes estimates his net worth at **$7 billion**.

### Crypto Thesis: The Institutional Bridge

Novogratz positioned Galaxy Digital as the **bridge between traditional finance and crypto**, offering institutional-grade services across trading, asset management, investment banking, and mining. His core thesis has evolved:

**Early thesis (2017-2023):** Bitcoin as digital gold, institutional adoption inevitable, crypto as macro hedge.

**Current thesis (2025-2026):** The "age of speculation" is ending; crypto's future lies in **tokenized real-world assets** with lower but more sustainable returns.

> "The age of speculation... will be phased out as the crypto industry has brought in institutions where people have a different risk tolerance." -- Mike Novogratz (CNBC, February 2026)

### Key Milestones & Developments

**Nasdaq Listing (May 2025):**
Galaxy spent **nearly four years and $25M+** trying to list in the United States. What should have taken 45-90 days stretched to **1,320 days** with nine rounds of SEC comments. The turning point came with Trump's election and the appointment of SEC Chair Paul Atkins. Galaxy now trades under **GLXY on Nasdaq**.

**Tokenization of Shares on Solana (September 2025):**
Galaxy became the **first publicly traded company to tokenize its SEC-registered equity** on a major blockchain. Through a partnership with Superstate, Galaxy's Class A common shares can be held on Solana with full shareholder rights. 21 investors tokenized 32,374 shares initially. Galaxy and Superstate are exploring regulatory-compliant AMM trading under the SEC's Project Crypto innovation agenda.

**$175M Venture Fund (June 2025):**
Galaxy raised a $175M venture fund (exceeding the $150M target) focused on the intersection of TradFi and crypto, particularly stablecoins and DeFi.

**Forward Industries Investment (September 2025):**
Galaxy participated in the $1.65B PIPE for Forward Industries' SOL-focused treasury strategy.

2. **Institutional Adoption Inevitability:** TradFi and crypto converge until "TradFi" is just "finance"
3. **Bitcoin as Hard Money:** Preserving Bitcoin's role as sound money amid monetary debasement

### Recent Activity (February-March 2026)

## 6. Cobie (Jordan Fish)

### Background & Career

Jordan Fish, known as **Cobie**, is a British crypto investor, analyst, and cultural figure. Born and raised in the UK, he studied computer science at the University of Bristol. His early interest in online communities, gaming, and technology led him into crypto during its early days.

Cobie's influence skyrocketed during the **DeFi boom of 2020** when he became one of Crypto Twitter's most followed and respected voices. His style combines deep market insight with irreverent humor, making complex crypto topics accessible and entertaining.

### The UpOnly Podcast

Co-founded with trader **Brian Krogsgard (Ledger)** during the 2021 bull run, UpOnly became a cultural touchstone for crypto insiders. The show featured interviews with top traders, builders, and meme legends, including Vitalik Buterin, CZ, and SBF (before his downfall).

The podcast was underwritten by **FTX**, and its final episode dropped shortly after FTX's implosion in November 2022. The show went dark for nearly three years.

**Revival via Coinbase (October 2025):** Coinbase purchased the UpOnly NFT for **$25 million in USDC**. The NFT's fine print required Cobie and Ledger to produce eight episodes. This works out to approximately **$1.5 million per episode** (if proceeds are split equally). Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong called it "a piece of crypto culture worth preserving."

### The Echo Platform & Coinbase Acquisition

Cobie founded **Echo**, an on-chain capital formation platform that helps communities invest together and gives founders options for their cap table. By October 2025, Echo had processed over **$200 million** in transactions across ~300 investments.

On **October 21, 2025**, Coinbase acquired Echo for approximately **$375 million** in cash and stock. Combined with the UpOnly NFT, Cobie's 2025 deals totaled roughly **$400 million**.

Strategic rationale for Coinbase:
- Echo handles fundraising (primary market)
- LiquiFi (acquired July 2025) manages post-issuance
- Coinbase's exchange handles secondary trading
- Together: a **full-stack solution** from launch to fundraising to trading

After the sale, Cobie immediately announced he would join **Paradigm as advisor**, focusing on liquid markets, trading, and DeFi trends.

### Trading Insights & Commentary

Cobie's market views are notable for their sobriety amid hype:
- **Markets in 2025-2026** will feel "more professional and less euphoric" than prior bull runs
- The next phase "won't just be about price cycles, but about real adoption, infrastructure, and survivorship"
- Known for calling tops and bottoms with contrarian thinking
- His 13-year journey from a $200 initial investment to $400M+ in exits is one of crypto's most remarkable individual stories

4. **Macro Skepticism:** "Macro is not going to ever give you an edge; never... Stick to what you know."
5. **Risk Timing:** Take more risk when markets are turning around; take less risk when prices are rising quickly.
6. **Simplicity:** If you don't have time for active trading, "you can probably beat most traders simply by holding bitcoin and ether."
