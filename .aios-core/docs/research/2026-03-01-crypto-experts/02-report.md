### The Stock-to-Flow Models

#### S2F (Stock-to-Flow) Model -- March 2019
- Quantifies Bitcoin's value based on scarcity (ratio of existing supply to new issuance)
- Treats Bitcoin as comparable to commodities (gold, silver, platinum)
- Core assumption: scarcity is the dominant price driver
- After each halving, the flow is cut in half, doubling the S2F ratio
- The model plots a logarithmic regression of S2F ratio vs. market cap

#### S2FX (Stock-to-Flow Cross-Asset) Model
- Enhanced version incorporating "clusters" representing different Bitcoin phases
- Adds market capitalization dimension
- Compares Bitcoin's progression across phases to how gold and silver behaved historically
- Allows identification of different price regimes

### Model Accuracy & Criticisms

**Strengths:**
- Correctly identified the directional trend of each post-halving cycle
- Early cycle predictions (2019-2021) were roughly aligned with actual prices
- Remains one of the most widely cited Bitcoin valuation frameworks

**Weaknesses:**
- Considers only supply, ignores demand, liquidity, and macro conditions
- Some cycle predictions were significantly above actual prices
- Does not account for evolved market structure (ETFs, institutional participation)
- Many now view it as "historical reference rather than reliable prediction model"
