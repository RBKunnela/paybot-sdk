## 1. Michael Saylor

### Background & Career

Michael Saylor (born 1965, Lincoln, Nebraska) grew up in a military family, moving between Air Force bases in the U.S., Japan, and New Zealand. He graduated first in his class from high school near Dayton, Ohio, and attended MIT on a full Air Force ROTC scholarship, earning dual degrees in aeronautics/astronautics and science, technology & society (1987). A benign heart murmur ended his jet-pilot aspirations, redirecting him to consulting.

After a $250,000 contract building a computer model for DuPont's titanium division, Saylor co-founded **MicroStrategy** in 1989 with MIT fraternity brother Sanju Bansal. The company pioneered relational online analytical processing (ROLAP) for business intelligence, went public in 1998 (NASDAQ: MSTR), and made Saylor the wealthiest man in the D.C. area with a $7B net worth by early 2000.

The dot-com crash hit hard: MicroStrategy restated two years of financials in March 2000, and the stock plummeted 62% in a single day. Saylor settled SEC charges for $8.3M without admitting wrongdoing. His net worth fell by $6 billion. He is also the inventor of 48+ patents, the creator of Alarm.com (NASDAQ: ALRM), and the author of *The Mobile Wave* (2012).

### Bitcoin Thesis: "Digital Energy" to "Digital Credit"

Saylor's crypto journey began in August 2020 during the pandemic, when he made MicroStrategy the first public company to adopt Bitcoin as a treasury reserve asset. His thesis has evolved through several phases:

**Phase 1 -- Digital Energy (2020-2024):**
Saylor frames Bitcoin as "digital energy" -- akin to fire, electricity, or oil -- capable of transferring value across time and space. He views Bitcoin not as speculative but as a foundational form of capital for the digital age, superior to gold, real estate, and bonds as a store of value.

**Phase 2 -- Digital Capital (2024-2025):**
He extended the framework to position Bitcoin as the base layer of a new financial system, calling it "digital capital" that appreciates faster than monetary debasement.

**Phase 3 -- Digital Credit (2026):**
At Strategy World 2026 (February), Saylor unveiled his most ambitious framework yet. He proposed that Bitcoin-backed credit instruments could capture 5-10% of the $300 trillion global credit market ($50-60T). The centerpiece is STRC, a Nasdaq-listed perpetual preferred security that converts Bitcoin volatility into stable income. He envisions up to 5,000 Bitcoin treasury companies operating in the U.S. alone.

### Key Frameworks & Mental Models

- **"21 Rules of Bitcoin"**: A framework for understanding Bitcoin's fixed supply, network effects, and adoption dynamics.
- **Bitcoin as Property**: Everything in the physical world degrades; Bitcoin is the first asset that is "immortal, immutable, and infinitely divisible."
- **Internal Risk Metrics**: Strategy uses three proprietary metrics -- BTC rating (collateral coverage), BTC risk (probability collateral falls below thresholds), and implied credit spread.
- **Orange Pill Philosophy**: Aggressive evangelism that Bitcoin education is a moral imperative.

## 3. Larry Fink

### Background & Career

Went public in 1999. Built the Aladdin risk management platform. ## 4. SEC Leadership: Gary Gensler (Legacy) & Paul Atkins (Current)

### Gary Gensler -- Background & Legacy (2021-2025)

Gary Gensler (born October 18, 1957) served as the 33rd SEC Chair from April 17, 2021 to January 20, 2025. Previously, he was an MIT professor teaching blockchain and digital currencies, Goldman Sachs partner, and CFTC Chair (2009-2014).

**Enforcement-First Approach:**

| Metric | Gensler Era | Clayton Era (Prior) |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Total Crypto Enforcement Actions | 125 | 70 |
| Total Penalties | $6.05 billion | $1.52 billion |
| Fraud Allegations | 66% of cases | 54% of cases |
| Broader SEC Actions | 2,700+ total | -- |
| Total SEC Penalties | ~$21 billion | -- |

**Major Enforcement Targets:**
1. **Binance/CZ**: $4.3B settlement (largest single action)
2. **Coinbase**: Sued June 2023 for unregistered securities
3. **Ripple/XRP**: $125M fine (August 2024)
4. **Kraken**: $30M penalty, ceased U.S. staking
5. **Terraform Labs**: $4.5B consent judgment

**Criticism:**
- "Regulation by enforcement" -- no clear frameworks, just lawsuits
- 18 Republican attorneys general sued the SEC for overreach
- Industry argued the 1946 Howey Test was ill-suited for decentralized protocols
- Many crypto firms relocated overseas due to regulatory uncertainty
- Dropped 30% in enforcement actions during final year (2024), with half of 33 cases filed Sept-Oct before the election

### Paul Atkins -- Current SEC Chair (2025-Present)

Paul Atkins was confirmed as the 34th SEC Chair in 2025, marking a radical departure from the Gensler era. Previously an SEC Commissioner (2002-2008), Atkins is known for his pro-business, deregulatory stance.

**"Project Crypto" Initiative:**

The centerpiece of Atkins's agenda is Project Crypto, which includes:

1. **Token Taxonomy (January 28, 2026)**: A four-category classification system:
   - Digital commodities
   - Digital collectibles
   - Digital tools
   - Tokenized securities
   - Includes a "sunset" provision ending security status once decentralization is proven

2. **Innovation Exemption**: A sandbox framework allowing companies to test novel business models under principles-based safeguards, with periodic SEC reporting in exchange for flexibility. (Initial rollout was delayed by the October 2025 government shutdown.)

3. **SEC-CFTC Harmonization (January 29, 2026)**: Joint event with CFTC Chairman Mike Selig, launching the most ambitious inter-agency collaboration in a generation. The CFTC will partner on Project Crypto for "coordination, coherence, and a unified approach."

4. **Clarity Act Alignment**: Atkins is pushing rules to align with the Clarity Act (crypto market structure legislation), which would give the CFTC jurisdiction over spot digital commodity markets and leave securities-based digital assets with the SEC.

**Key Statements:**
- Most crypto tokens "do not qualify as securities under existing law"
- Regulatory clarity, not litigation, should define the landscape
- Federal structure on crypto markets is "long overdue"

**Political Controversy:**
- Rep. Maxine Waters criticized abandonment of nearly all prior crypto enforcement cases
- Sen. Elizabeth Warren condemned weakening enforcement, raised donor influence concerns
- At a February 13, 2026 Senate hearing, Atkins was grilled on the enforcement pull-back

**Historic First:** Atkins will be the first sitting SEC Chair to speak at the Bitcoin Conference (Las Vegas, April 27-29, 2026).

## 5. Vitalik Buterin

### Background & Early Life

Vitaly Dmitrievich Buterin (born January 31, 1994, Kolomna, Russia) is a Russian-Canadian computer programmer. His father Dmitry was a computer scientist. The family emigrated to Canada when Vitalik was six. He was placed in a gifted program in elementary school and displayed exceptional aptitude in mathematics, programming, and economics (estimated IQ ~160).

At 17, his father introduced him to Bitcoin. Initially skeptical, he became fascinated on second exposure. In 2011, he co-founded **Bitcoin Magazine** with Mihai Alisie, becoming one of the earliest crypto journalists. He won a bronze medal at the International Olympiad in Informatics (2012) and briefly attended the University of Waterloo, where he was a research assistant for cryptographer Ian Goldberg (co-creator of OTR messaging, former Tor board chair).

**Famous motivation:** "I happily played World of Warcraft during 2007-2010, but one day Blizzard removed the damage component from my beloved warlock's Siphon Life spell. I cried myself to sleep, and on that day I realized what horrors centralized services can bring."

### Creation of Ethereum

In November 2013, Buterin published the Ethereum white paper after failing to convince Bitcoin developers to add a scripting language. He presented Ethereum publicly at the North American Bitcoin Conference in Miami (January 2014). With co-founders Gavin Wood, Joseph Lubin, Anthony Di Iorio, and Charles Hoskinson, he raised 31,000 BTC (~$18M) via an ICO in 2014.

He dropped out of university after receiving a $100,000 Thiel Fellowship in 2014. Ethereum's first version (Frontier) launched on July 30, 2015.

**Key achievements:**
- Thiel Fellowship (2014)
- World Technology Award in Software (2015)
- Fortune "40 Under 40" (2016)
- Honorary Doctorate, University of Basel (2018)
- Donated $1B+ in crypto to India COVID Relief Fund (2021)

### Ethereum Roadmap (2025-2026)

Buterin's current roadmap centers on several "surges":

**1. Scaling (The Surge):**
- Proposed making Ethereum up to 1,000x more powerful via zkEVM-based validation
- Published "Glamsterdam" upgrade plan (Feb 2026) for more efficient block checking
- Advocated making permanent data storage more expensive while increasing transaction throughput
- Gas reform: not all activity puts equal strain on the network
- Timeline: zkEVM verification experiments in 2026, broader adoption by 2027

**2. Quantum Resistance (Feb 26, 2026):**
Four key vulnerability areas identified:
- **Consensus signatures**: Switch to hash-based signatures
- **Data storage**: Replace KZG commitments with quantum-safe alternatives
- **User wallets**: EIP-8141 for flexible signature schemes
- **Proof aggregation**: "Validation frames" to bundle proofs

**3. 2025 Technical Achievements:**
- Gas limit increased from 30M to 36M units
- Blob capacity expanded from 3 to 6 (Pectra upgrade)
- zkEVM proving times reduced from 16 minutes to 16 seconds (60x improvement)

**4. Philosophical Direction:**
- "Walkaway test" -- systems should continue running regardless of who maintains them
- Renewed commitment to Ethereum as a "world computer" -- neutral, censorship-resistant
- Proposed replacing EVM with RISC-V architecture for "up to 100%" efficiency gains

### Ethereum Foundation Changes (2025-2026)

The Foundation underwent major restructuring:
- Tomasz Stanczak appointed Co-Executive Director (2025), then stepped down (February 2026)
- Hsiao-Wei Wang joined EF leadership
- Buterin freed from day-to-day coordination to focus on pure research
- Foundation focus: L1 scaling, L2 support, user experience
- Upcoming upgrades: Pectra, Fusaka, Glamsterdam, Hegota (second major 2026 fork)
- Foundation will NOT engage in political lobbying

## 6. Charles Hoskinson

### Background & Career

Charles Hoskinson (born November 5, 1987, Maui, Hawaii) grew up in a family of physicians but pursued mathematics instead. He attended Metropolitan State University of Denver and the University of Colorado Boulder as an undergraduate math major (did not complete a degree, though he has claimed PhD-level studies). His academic credentials have been a point of controversy.

Hoskinson's crypto journey began through Bitcoin and libertarian politics -- he worked as a fundraiser for Ron Paul's 2008 presidential campaign. He quit his consulting job in 2013 to go "all-in" on crypto, became the founding chairman of the Bitcoin Foundation's education committee, and helped establish the Cryptocurrency Research Group.

### Ethereum Co-founding & Departure

Hoskinson was one of Ethereum's five original co-founders (late 2013), serving briefly as CEO. He pushed for a for-profit corporate structure funded by venture capital, while Vitalik Buterin favored a non-profit foundation. This "boardroom brawl" ended when the founders let Buterin decide. Hoskinson was removed in 2014.

### Input Output Global (IOG) & Cardano

After a six-month sabbatical, Hoskinson co-founded **Input Output Hong Kong (IOHK, now IOG)** in March 2015 with fellow Ethereum co-founder Jeremy Wood. Their flagship project, **Cardano**, launched in 2017 -- originally focused on the Japanese market before expanding globally. The ADA cryptocurrency is Cardano's native token.

**Academic Approach:**
IOG's distinctive methodology involves peer-reviewed research published in academic journals before implementation. Partnerships with the University of Edinburgh, Tokyo Institute of Technology, Stanford University, and the University of Wyoming. This approach prioritizes mathematical verification and formal methods.

### Midnight Privacy Blockchain (March 2026 Launch)

Midnight is IOG's most anticipated project, launching in the final week of March 2026:

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Technology | Zero-knowledge proofs for selective disclosure |
| Consensus | Jolteon protocol -- 5,000 TPS, sub-second blocks |
| Relationship to Cardano | Partner chain (not competing) |
| Investment | ~$1 billion project |
| Partners | Google, Telegram, Tier-1 exchanges |
| Privacy Philosophy | "Smart curtain" -- privacy by default, invisible to users |
| Target Users | Billions who "don't know they need privacy" (NOT Monero/ZCash users) |

**Phased Rollout:**
- Q1 2026: Kukolu phase -- developer mainnet for privacy-focused dApps
- Q2-Q3 2026: Mohalu and Hua phases -- incentivized testnet, hybrid dApps

### Cardano Ecosystem Developments

**Scaling:**
- Leios scaling protocol in development (expanding faster than any prior Cardano effort)
- Hydra L2 solution supports Glacier Drop, Delta DeFi
- StarStream, Plutus V4, Aiken -- base layer throughput improvements

**RealFi (Real-World Finance):**
- Micro-lending platform with 1M+ loans issued in Kenya and Uganda
- Tests credit models with microfinance institutions
- Hoskinson projects it will become the largest driver of TVL and transaction volume

**Governance:**
- On-chain constitution and decentralized decision-making already operational
- Upgrades proceed without reliance on central authority

**AI Security Warning:**
Hoskinson warned that AI may identify zero-day blockchain exploits within 2-3 years. Cardano's formal methods (mathematical verification) provide a defensive advantage.

### Personal Life & Exits

Hoskinson owns an 11,000-acre buffalo ranch near Wheatland, Wyoming, and a farm in Boulder, Colorado. He opened the Hoskinson Health and Wellness Clinic ($18M) in Gillette, Wyoming, focusing on anti-aging medicine. He donated $20M to Carnegie Mellon University (2021).

## 7. Anatoly Yakovenko

### Background & Career

**Career before Solana:**

### Founding of Solana

The inspiration came one night in late 2017 after two coffees and a beer. Yakovenko realized that **the passage of time itself** could serve as a data structure to order blockchain transactions -- the insight that became **Proof of History (PoH)**. This was inspired by time division multiple access (TDMA) technology from his Qualcomm days, where cellular towers alternate transmissions based on time intervals, combined with Stanford's verifiable delay function research.

He published the Solana whitepaper in February 2018, and co-founded Solana Labs with Qualcomm alumni Greg Fitzgerald, Stephen Akridge, and Raj Gokal. Solana officially launched in March 2020.

### Technical Architecture & Vision

**Core Innovation: Proof of History (PoH)**
PoH creates a verifiable, cryptographic timestamp before consensus, allowing the network to process transactions without waiting for agreement on ordering. This enables theoretical throughput of 50,000+ TPS with sub-second finality.

**L1 Scaling Philosophy:**
Yakovenko's defining technical conviction is that scaling should happen at Layer 1, not through "fragmented" Layer 2 ecosystems. He argues that hardware improvements (TSMC, Nvidia advances) will naturally increase throughput over time.

## 8. Hayden Adams

## 9. Do Kwon (Historical Context)

### Background & Career

Kwon Do-hyung (born September 6, 1991, South Korea) graduated in Computer Engineering from Stanford University. He worked briefly at Apple and Microsoft before founding Anyfi (decentralized communication networks) in 2016.

In 2018, he co-founded **Terraform Labs** with Daniel Shin, a seasoned South Korean tech entrepreneur. Their mission: build a decentralized payment system powered by an algorithmic stablecoin to reduce volatility in crypto payments.

### The Algorithmic Stablecoin Model

**TerraUSD (UST)** launched in September 2020 as an algorithmic stablecoin pegged to the dollar without fiat collateral. It used a dual-token "burn and mint equilibrium" system:
- Burning 1 LUNA = minting $1 of UST
- Burning $1 of UST = minting 1 LUNA
- This created an arbitrage loop designed to maintain the peg

The **Anchor Protocol** promised ~20% yields on UST deposits, driving explosive growth. By early 2022, the combined market cap of UST and LUNA exceeded $50 billion.

### The Collapse (May 2022)

In May 2022, UST lost its dollar peg, triggering a catastrophic "death spiral":
1. As UST fell below $1, the algorithm minted LUNA at exponential rates to restore the peg
2. This hyperinflated LUNA's supply, crashing its value to near-zero
3. ~$40 billion in market value was erased in approximately one week
4. The contagion spread: Three Arrows Capital, Celsius, Voyager, and BlockFi all collapsed in the aftermath

### Legal Proceedings

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| March 2023 | Arrested in Montenegro with falsified travel documents |
| April 2024 | Jury found Terraform Labs and Kwon liable (2-hour deliberation) |
| June 2024 | SEC settlement: $4.5B consent judgment |
| August 2025 | Pleaded guilty to 2 counts of fraud (7 other charges dropped) |
| December 11, 2025 | **Sentenced to 15 years in federal prison** |

**Sentencing Details:**
- Prosecutors sought 12 years; defense requested 5 years
- Judge Paul Engelmayer imposed 15 years -- exceeding both recommendations
- Called it "fraud on epic, generational scale"
- Financial penalties: $3.59B disgorgement, $467M interest, $420M civil penalties
- Kwon must contribute $204M+ to Terraform bankruptcy estate, including all Luna Foundation Guard crypto assets
- 16,500+ investors sought restitution
- Kwon will be ~48 years old when eligible for parole
- Potential subsequent extradition to South Korea (up to 40 additional years)

### Impact on Crypto Regulation

The Terra/Luna collapse was arguably the single most consequential event in accelerating crypto regulation:
- Directly contributed to the EU's MiCA regulatory framework
- Intensified Gensler's SEC enforcement campaign
- Prompted global scrutiny of algorithmic stablecoins
- Forced exchanges to implement proof-of-reserves
- Led to multiple countries banning or restricting algorithmic stablecoin designs

### Lessons Learned

1. **Algorithmic stablecoins carry inherent death-spiral risk** when relying solely on endogenous collateral
2. **Unsustainable yields** (Anchor's ~20%) are red flags signaling subsidized growth
3. **Founder credibility** is not a substitute for transparent, auditable mechanisms
4. **Contagion risk** in interconnected crypto ecosystems can cascade rapidly
5. **Regulatory gaps** allowed a $50B system to operate without meaningful oversight
