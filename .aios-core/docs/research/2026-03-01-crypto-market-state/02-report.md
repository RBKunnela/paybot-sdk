## 4. Regulatory Landscape

### 4.1 US Crypto Regulation (SEC, CFTC)

The US regulatory landscape has undergone a dramatic transformation under SEC Chair **Paul Atkins**:

**SEC "Project Crypto" initiative:**
- Forthcoming "Regulation Crypto" with 2026 rulemakings for a comprehensive framework
- Amendments for crypto trading on exchanges
- Guidance on tokenized securities
- Market structure for "super-apps"
- "Innovation exemption" for entrepreneurs to enter markets without burdensome pre-compliance

**SEC enforcement shift:**
- Dropped nearly all enforcement actions commenced under Biden (those without fraud allegations)
- Suspended 12+ major crypto enforcement cases, including Justin Sun/Tron
- Shifted from enforcement-heavy crypto-skepticism to flexibility for market participants

**CFTC partnership:** Chairman Michael Selig announced (January 29, 2026) that the CFTC will partner with the SEC on Project Crypto, bringing "coordination, coherence, and a unified approach."

**Key legislation:**
- **GENIUS Act** (signed July 18, 2025): First comprehensive federal stablecoin legislation
- **CLARITY Act**: Passed House in July 2025; awaits Senate action. Grants CFTC jurisdiction over digital commodity spot markets
- **Implementing rules due:** July 18, 2026 for GENIUS Act regulations; effective January 18, 2027

### 4.2 Stablecoin Legislation

**The GENIUS Act (Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for US Stablecoins Act):**
- Signed: July 18, 2025
- Requires 1:1 backing with highly liquid assets (USD, short-term Treasuries)
- Mandatory AML/sanctions compliance
- Stablecoins are explicitly NOT securities, commodities, or deposits
- Federal oversight by OCC, FDIC, Federal Reserve Board, and state banking regulators
- Implementing rules due: **July 18, 2026**

**SEC capital treatment:** New guidance applies only a 2% haircut to qualifying stablecoins, counting 98% toward regulatory capital -- a significant step toward using stablecoins in core market functions.

**Hot debate:** Banks vs. native crypto companies over whether customer "reward" programs circumvent the GENIUS Act's prohibition on paying interest/yield on stablecoins.

**Stablecoin market cap:** $317.9B as of January 2026, up from $205B at start of 2025.

**USDT vs. USDC dynamics:**
- USDT: $183B market cap, dominant but contracting (6.5B burned in Jan-Feb 2026; non-compliant with EU MiCA)
- USDC: $75B market cap, growing 73% YoY; full MiCA compliance; Circle beat earnings estimates

### 4.3 EU MiCA Implementation

The Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) is entering full enforcement in 2026:

- **Full application deadline:** Mid-2026 (no further grace periods)
- **CASP licensing:** ~130-140 licenses issued EU-wide (Germany, Netherlands, France, Malta leading)
- **Passporting:** Authorization in one EU country grants access to all 27 member states
- **Capital requirements:** EUR 50K (advisory), EUR 125K (custody/exchange), EUR 150K (trading platforms)
- **Early results:** ECB reported 60% decline in crypto fraud; 30%+ of institutional investors increased exposure; retail participation grew 27%

**Key licensees:** Coinbase, Bitstamp, Clearstream (Luxembourg); Commerzbank, N26, Trade Republic (Germany)

**Additional EU frameworks:**
- **AMLA** (EU AML Authority) beginning supervisory convergence
- **Transfer of Funds Regulation:** Travel rule fully embedded in crypto transfers by 2026
- **DORA** (Digital Operational Resilience Act): Applies to MiCA-licensed crypto firms since January 2025

**February 2026 improvement:**
- Losses dropped to **$37.7 million** -- lowest monthly figure since March 2025

- Top incidents: YieldBlox ($10.6M), IoTeX ($8.9M), Foom ($2.3M)
