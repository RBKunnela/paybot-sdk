# Next Steps & Recommendations

## Actionable Recommendations

### For Investors / Portfolio Strategy

1. **Monitor the Fed Chair transition closely (May 2026).** Kevin Warsh's first policy signals will define the rate trajectory for 2H 2026 and beyond. Position for increased volatility around the transition window.

2. **Stablecoins are infrastructure, not speculation.** The $27T annual volume and 54% expected corporate adoption rate make stablecoin infrastructure plays (Circle, Stripe integrations, stablecoin-as-a-service providers) a high-conviction theme.

3. **RWA tokenization is the largest structural alpha.** With $25B on-chain today and $100B+ projected by year-end, tokenized treasuries, private credit, and gold represent the clearest growth vector. Watch Ethereum's 65% market share in this space.

4. **Bitcoin's risk/reward is asymmetric but uncertain.** The $60K-$70K range could represent either a re-accumulation zone before a move to $150K+ or the beginning of a deeper cycle correction to $35K-$55K. The halving cycle's predictive power has diminished; focus on ETF flow data and institutional lending metrics instead.

5. **Hedge midterm election year risk.** Historical data shows 12/17 midterm cycles produce corrections averaging 17.5%. Consider options strategies or cash allocation increases in Q2-Q3 2026.

### For Builders / Developers

6. **Build for AI agents as customers.** The convergence of AI and crypto is shifting from theoretical to practical. Stablecoin-based micropayment rails, on-chain identity for agents, and x402-style protocols represent concrete building opportunities.

7. **Cross-chain intents > traditional bridges.** ERC-7683 and the Open Intents Framework are becoming the standard. New protocols should adopt intent-based architecture over lock/mint patterns.

8. **BTCFi is early but real.** Babylon Protocol's $4B in staked BTC proves demand. Trust-minimized bridges (BitVM2, Starknet-Alpen partnership) reduce the biggest friction point. Building on Bitcoin's emerging DeFi stack is a contrarian bet with institutional backing.

9. **Privacy is a feature, not a product.** ZKPs and FHE should be integrated into existing products (lending, identity, compliance) rather than built as standalone privacy chains. Institutional demand is for selective disclosure, not full anonymity.

10. **Prepare for Glamsterdam and Alpenglow.** Ethereum's Glamsterdam (early 2026) and Solana's Alpenglow (2026 target) both offer significant performance improvements. Building applications that leverage these upgrades before they go live creates first-mover advantage.

### For Policy Watchers

11. **Track the CLARITY Act implementation.** Expected to come into effect in 2026, this legislation standardizes definitions of digital commodities vs. securities and will reshape which tokens can be listed where.

12. **Watch China's e-CNY integration with mBridge.** The US withdrawal from retail CBDC research has ceded ground to China. Project mBridge connecting BRICS central banks is a tangible de-dollarization infrastructure with 2026 milestones.

13. **US Strategic Bitcoin Reserve execution gaps.** The gap between the executive order's ambition (1M BTC target) and the reality (328K forfeited coins, no active purchases) creates both policy risk and potential catalyst if purchases begin.
